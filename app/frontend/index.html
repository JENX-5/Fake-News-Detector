Methodology

A six-stage research pipeline

Two datasets. Seven models. Six evaluation metrics. Full explainability and uncertainty quantification.

01

Preprocessing

12-stage NLP pipeline applied to both ISOT and WELFake: dateline removal, HTML decoding, contraction expansion, negation marking, stopword filtering, WordNet lemmatisation. DistilBERT receives raw text — the pipeline feeds classical models only.

02

Feature Extraction

TF-IDF with 10,000 unigram and bigram features, sublinear TF scaling. Augmented by 11 stylometric signals: capitalisation ratio, exclamation density, type-token ratio, negation frequency, and more.

03

Classical Models

Six classifiers benchmarked under 5-fold cross-validation: Multinomial NB, Complement NB, Logistic Regression, Linear SVM, Random Forest, and a soft-voting Ensemble. Linear SVM leads at F1 = 0.9891 on ISOT.

04

DistilBERT Fine-Tuning

66M-parameter transformer fine-tuned for 3 epochs with AdamW + linear warmup on Apple Silicon MPS. Achieves F1 = 0.9997 on ISOT — a cross-dataset degradation of only −0.0064, five times smaller than Linear SVM.

05

Explainability

LIME for MNB local explanations. SHAP TreeExplainer for Random Forest global feature importance. Token-level attention heatmaps for DistilBERT, treated as correlational indicators per Jain & Wallace (2019).

06

Uncertainty Quantification

Monte Carlo Dropout with 20 stochastic inference passes. Mean P(Real) and standard deviation across passes estimate epistemic uncertainty. Incorrect predictions exhibit measurably higher uncertainty — validating MC-Dropout as a human-review signal.

Research Disclaimer — TruthLens is trained on ISOT (US political news, 2016–2018) and WELFake (multi-source aggregated). It is a research demonstration, not a production fact-checking system. Automated detection should supplement — never replace — independent critical judgement. ECE ≈ 0.41 across all models indicates raw probabilities require post-hoc calibration before deployment.
